# Chapter 06: Conclusion

The objective of this work was to develop a platform to manage Amazon
Store Business Intelligence and Inventory Management using Streamlit.
This work aimed at transforming product data gathered in Amazon from CSV
files into valuable business information, which would be useful for
order processing, inventory management, analytics, and reporting via one
single web application. Rather than having customers and employees use
the raw product data stored in the form of CSV files, the application
processes such data into useful business information through cleaning,
transformation, visualizations, and dashboarding.

The platform created in this study included two interfaces. The first
one allows customers to search for a product and filter by categories,
sort by relevance, choose the quantity and confirm orders only if there
are sufficient stocks available. Such interface makes it possible to
improve the experience of ordering items from Amazon and reduce the risk
of receiving an invalid order. The second interface is used for
employees to perform different analyses. It includes inventory
dashboarding, stock alert, demand analysis, ABC classification, EOQ
calculation, KPIs tracking, transaction logging, and executive
reporting.

During the discussion, it was shown that the use of the proposed BI
platform would make managing inventory and analyzing product data easier
for Amazon staff and managers. Without the use of the BI tool, the
product and inventory data were mostly processed manually in the form of
CSV files. Such situation was inconvenient in terms of identifying
shortage products, surplus stocks, highly demanded items, and sales
opportunities. Applying the BI logic suggested in this project helped
turn raw CSV data into a well-structured data set. Expected changes were
confirmed in the comparison.

Finally, this project proved the importance of applying Business
Intelligence concepts in retail and e-commerce environments. The use of
demand score, estimation of monthly demand rate, reorder point, stock
level, revenue generation potential, profit opportunity, ABC analysis,
and EOQ cannot be limited to mathematical calculations. These concepts
and metrics have to be presented through charts and dashboards, which
make interpreting and analyzing data much easier for users.

While being just a prototype, the platform is a great basis for further
work. The existing platform relies on the usage of CSV files and demand
forecasts, which is quite adequate for a student BI project. In its
future implementations, one might include the creation of a proper
database, user authorization, information about suppliers, lead time
calculation features, payments simulation, more complex models for
forecasting, and machine learning tools utilizing historical
transactions.

To summarize, it is possible to state that the current work demonstrates
the capabilities of Python and Streamlit in creating a professional BI
platform for e-commerce inventory management. Such a platform provides
not only attractive visuals but also allows for faster purchases,
improved control of inventory, performance tracking, and making
business-related decisions. As a result, the Amazon Store BI Platform is
an important case study of the potential of using data analytics for
improving modern business systems' efficiency.
